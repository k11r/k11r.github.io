* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *
The Feng Shui Fun Club is an e-mailing list based weekly content package. The first issue was sent on Sunday 1/4/2015 at 12:46 AM from fengshuifriends@hotmail.com. Then, the mailing list included four people (Carson Cooper, Mick Robbins, Brian Cordes, and Alyssa Nunez (The infamous buttsbuttsbutts email that started off every ‚Äúsent to:‚Äù box,) Since then, the email list has grown to include over 45 people. These emails included a brief text segment, a .ZIP file, and links to external downloads and the FSFC directory.

The content included in these e-packages consists first and foremost of images and text files with web links. This content could be hosted on a blog, however, the dynamic of folder order plays a unique part in how the content is organized. The classic fun club has a handful of good pictures in the main folder, followed by other folders containing more bizarre or funny images. Usually there is a text file at the top or end of these with a smattering of web links. However, in the case of the Fantasy Lunchbox series, or one-offs like Plastic Map and #5 Portal, the folder organization system becomes a virtual labyrinth to explore and discover hidden gems within. Because of this reliance on folder organization, the Feng Shui Fun Club cannot properly be manifested in any other form besides a .ZIP file.

The art associated with the Fun Club started out with simple ASCII rips. Gathered and lightly tweaked from various ascii art pages, most of these early virtual flyers ended up being sent out through the filter of the hotmail platform. The hotmail color palette was very limited, and a combination of pastel yellows, greens, blues, and pinks was often used. Also, detached from any monospaced fonts, these designs frequently shifted around and distorted when opened on other email clients.

Below are the virtual flyers, promotional images, and original screenshots from the first Fun Clubs.
