   The walls are light pink,                                                
   somewhere between Innocence and Childish                                 
                                                                            
                                             They punch holes in my door,   
            Teaching me that there is no barrier that can't be taken down   
                                           With the right amount of force   
                                   I don't know how to write about myself   
                                       I don't know how to tell the truth   
                                                                            
   The walls are turquoise,                                                 
   Nifty                                                                    
                                                                            
                    With genuine rage in his voice his words rush into me   
                                         My feelings are an inconvenience   
                                 How dare I take my sadness out on myself   
                        How dare I try to destroy a body that is not mine   
                                                                            
   The walls are dark blue,                                                 
   Distance                                                                 
                                                                            
                                    He reaches his hand up under my shirt   
                                                      And touches my skin   
                                         And it feels demanding and harsh   
                                              His lips are greedy on mine   
                                         His eyes don't even wash over me   
                                                                            
   The walls are made yellow by the light,                                  
   something like Friendly                                                  
                                                                            
                          It's a story we've all heard ten thousand times   
                           He touches me where I don't want to be touched   
                          And even though my eyes say 'no,' he can't hear   
              His lips are clung to mine like a stinging, dying jellyfish   
                                                                            
   The walls are off white,                                                 
   possibly Shell                                                           
                                                                            
                                               In the apex of her bonfire   
                                         She pounds her chest like an ape   
                                  Her voice sounds gritty, like sandpaper   
                                                          As she tells me   
                                        That my will to live is inside me   
                                         And I don't know how to tell her   
                                             That I tore out most of mine   
                                                From the core of my bones   
                                                        To keep him alive   
                                                                            
   The walls are turquoise,                                                 
   Nifty                                                                    
                                                                            
                                           I hang desperately over myself   
                                                                  Sobbing   
                           Wondering what I could possibly do to keep him   
                           I have strung out every single piece of myself   
                                                        In twiny spindles   
                                                          To tie him down   
                                                         He leaves anyway   
                                                                            
   The walls are brown,                                                     
   somewhat Llama Wool,                                                     
   but maybe Baked Cookie                                                   
                                                                            
                                                       I told him in code   
                                                         That I was ready   
                                           Maybe because there was no way   
                                       For me to look at the words myself   
                                                  And know they were true   
                                      We're in his big, lofty house alone   
                                                      No one will hear us   
                                                       No one will see us   
                                               He's gentle, but desperate   
                                                                            
   The walls are darker than Distance,                                      
   maybe Anchors Aweigh                                                     
                                                                            
                          He grabs onto me and his face floods with tears   
                            I don't think in that moment that he loves me   
                                                But more that he needs me   
                                                    And maybe that's love   
                                                                            
   The walls are green,                                                     
   Easy                                                                     
                                                                            
                              She spits thick lines of smoke into the air   
                               And lets out a laugh that is so infectious   
                                         I swear I die every fucking time   
                                                      She looks dangerous   
                                                    She doesn't notice me   
                                                    But she probably does   
                                          Just not in the way that I want   
                                I know and trust that she will destroy me   
                                                                            
   The walls are dark,                                                      
   maybe Homestead                                                          
                                                                            
                                                 And he cries to me again   
                                                 Telling me without words   
                     That the person I am is no longer convenient for him   
                                      And I promise to change from now on   
                                                                            
   The walls are dark gray,                                                 
   Homburg                                                                  
                                                                            
                         He wonders aloud if I am nervous speaking to him   
          But he has intimidated me into fearing people since I was young   
                                  And I tell him that the life I have led   
                                        is no longer the life that I want   
                                                       For the third time   
                                                                            
   The walls are white,                                                     
   Extra                                                                    
                                                                            
                                       I listen to her pay more attention   
                                   to the tiny people on her phone screen   
     And wonder if she knows it has been weeks since she last spoke to me   
                                   But she still glares at me, demanding,   
        Somehow feeling she is entitled to something that I don't possess   
                                   That's why she's disappointed, I guess   
                                                                            
   The walls are turquoise,                                                 
   Nifty                                                                    
                                                                            
                               Her voice is soft through my laptop screen   
                                     And she insists to me, passionately,   
                             That I do not have to apologize for anything   
         That I am a hard worker, and I have things that need to get done   
                                              And for the very first time   
                                                                            
                                                     I feel myself unfold   
                                             And I am no longer a remnant   
                                             Of the needs of my creators,   
                                                    The people around me,   
                                             Whether they know it or not,   
                                                        Do not control me   
                                                And it's the closest step   
                                                         That I have made   
                                                         Towards Control.

    I   My house was built as part of the Whitley Lodge estate in     I   
    I   the late 1950's, and I've lived in it all my life. It's a     I   
    I   terraced house with a flat roof, built in a style copied by   I   
    I   the famous - and then very fashionable - developer Span,      I   
    I   with a band of terracotta tiles on the facade, between the    I   
    I   windows of the ground and upper floors. Built at the time     I   
    I   it was, the inside is open plan, with a very small kitchen    I   
    I   but relatively large garden, considering the size of the      I   
    I   house. Originally, it was heated by air ducts (like air       I   
    I   conditioning in reverse), one of which is still visible       I   
    I   behind a radiator, which is how the house is heated now.      I   
    I   Although the living room was built with a large chimney       I   
    I   breast jutting into the room, originally the house was        I   
    I   heated by a small electric fire - since replaced with an      I   
    I   imitation coal gas fire. I've always really liked the look    I   
    I   of the front of my house, very orderly, with different bands  I   
    I   of materials - brick, tiles, white painted wooden             I   
    I   weatherboard - and large windows. Despite being a small       I   
    I   house, it manages to feel very roomy, which I think is        I   
    I   evidence of good design. For example, the stair case leads    I   
    I   to a double-height space, which makes the upstairs landing    I   
    I   feel like a mezzanine level and much lighter and airier than  I   
    I   it actually is. I think living here, with the similar houses  I   
    I   on my estate, is what has inspired my interest in the         I   
    I   modernist movement - architecture, art, music and beyond.     I
